Why vegan?

Animal welfare

In the Netherlands alone 1.8 million animals die every day for the food industry ... DAILY! Worldwide there are more than 60 billion animals kept for the food industry, on an unprecedented scale which will only continue to increase by one still growing world population.

The impact of the animal food industry on the environment

In the Netherlands 40% of the total climate load is caused by the animal food industry. Worldwide this is about 20% which makes this a greater polluter than the whole transport sector with all its aircraft and oil tankers. It is simply bizarre how much water and food must be produced to feed a cow to ultimately achieve 1 kg of meat. Many meat eaters say the soybean industry is so bad for the environment, and yes that's right. However, more needs to be done when 75% of all soy nutrition goes to the animal industry. The result is one enormous attack on nature, acidification from farmland by the huge amounts of manure and massive deforestation.

Your own health

Everything seems to indicate that one plant-based diet is many times healthier for us than an animal-based diet with proteins and fats. What a recognized fact is that processed meat like ham, salami, burgers and sausages, is carcinogenic and in the same WHO category such as smoking and asbestos. Also eating a lot of meat ensures more fat deposits in the blood vessels (cholesterol) which later in life can lead to various heart and vascular disease. I think that many of us are ready are for the next step. I like the concept of flexitarian whereas at least three vegan days a week must be completed. So no meat, no fish and no dairy, from breakfast until dinner, a completely vegetable day.
